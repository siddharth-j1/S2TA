Safe & Semantically-Aware Trajectory Adaptation for Robot Manipulation via Natural Language

Shreyas Kumar, Siddharth Jain, and Ravi Prakash
Human-Interactive Robotics Lab, IISc Bangalore

S2TA executing a smooth trajectory around a soft obstacle while satisfying human-specified temporal logic constraints.

Abstract

Robotic manipulation in human environments requires satisfying geometric safety constraints together with high-level semantic requirements involving temporal ordering, timing, and object-dependent motion preferences expressed in natural language. We present a framework for language-conditioned trajectory synthesis that compiles semantic task specifications into certified motion and impedance policies.

Natural language instructions are translated into a typed representation expressed in weighted temporal logic over a structured predicate library. A deterministic compiler generates hard constraints enforced by construction through a feasible-manifold parameterization that guarantees collision avoidance, actuator feasibility, and impedance stability, together with a robustness-based objective capturing soft semantic preferences and temporal requirements. The resulting optimization synthesizes full six-degree-of-freedom trajectories and time-varying impedance schedules, enabling principled trade-offs between competing objectives while preserving hard guarantees. Hardware experiments demonstrate language-driven semantic reasoning, timing adaptation, and certified physically consistent manipulation behaviors.

Three-Layer Architecture

Our method decouples trajectory shape generation, impedance gain scheduling, and absolute geometric safety into independent, verifiable layers:

  • Layer 1 (Soft Geometric Routing): Injects a continuous repulsive force directly into the DMP ODE for smooth, natural obstacle avoidance.
  • Layer 2 (Certified Gain Scheduling): Guarantees unconditionally stable stiffness profiles ($K \succ 0$) via Cholesky dynamics ($\dot{Q}$).
  • Layer 3 (Unconditional Geometric Safety): A hard radial projector acts as an absolute safety backstop for safety-critical regions.
